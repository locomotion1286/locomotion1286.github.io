        <h2 style="color: white; font-size: 50px;">Uranus</h2>
        <p style="font-family: 'monospace'; color: #ffffff; font-size: 30px;">
     
Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, stands out for its unique tilt and icy composition. It is often referred to as 
an "ice giant" due to its predominantly icy mantle surrounding a rocky core. What sets Uranus apart is its extreme axial 
tilt, with its rotational axis nearly parallel to its orbital plane, causing it to appear to roll on its side as it orbits
 the Sun. This peculiar tilt results in extreme seasonal variations, with each pole experiencing 42 years of continuous daylight 
 followed by 42 years of darkness during its 84-year orbit. Despite its distant location, Uranus has a ring system and a collection
  of moons, with the most notable being Titania and Oberon. Uranus remains relatively unexplored compared to other gas giants, 
  with limited spacecraft visits such as Voyager 2 providing valuable but brief glimpses into its mysterious atmosphere and 
  features. Further exploration and study of Uranus could unlock secrets about the formation and evolution of our solar system and beyond.

           
        </p>
    

            <h3 style="font-size: 40px;">Fun Facts about Uranus</h3>
            <ul>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">It is an ice giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">Uranus has a unique feature of rotating on its side, with its axis tilted at an angle of about 98 degrees.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">The planet has a bluish-green color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">Uranus has a ring system, although not as prominent as Saturn's.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">It has 27 known moons, with the largest ones being Titania, Oberon, Umbriel, Ariel, and Miranda.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">Uranus takes about 84 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">The planet's atmosphere is composed of hydrogen, helium, and traces of methane.</li>
                <li style="font-size: 20px; color: #ffffff;">Uranus has a lower gravity compared to Earth, with a surface gravity about 0.89 times that of Earth.</li>
            </ul>

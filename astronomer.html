Ask An Astronomer

Q. How big is the universe?

A. The observable universe is about 93 billion light-years in diameter, constantly expanding.

Q. What's a black hole, and can anything escape from it?

A. A black hole is an extremely dense region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape its grasp.

Q. How do stars form?

A. Stars form from massive clouds of gas and dust. Gravity pulls the material together until nuclear fusion ignites, creating a star.

Q. Are there other planets like Earth in the universe?

A. Scientists believe there are many exoplanets in the habitable zone, where conditions might support life, but confirming it is a complex task.

Q. What causes a supernova, and can it affect us on Earth?

A. A supernova is the explosive death of a massive star. Fortunately, the nearest potential supernova is far enough away to pose no threat to Earth.
